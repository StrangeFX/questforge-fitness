Two Registers, One Language

Wood is the core. Violet is not removed — it is re-cast as the second of two registers that share a spine. The board is the waking world: physical, warm, where you stand and take a quest. The vellum is the journey: nocturnal, magical, where distance and time are drawn. Both are framed in the same wood.

The bridge

The frame is the shared element. Every surface in the app — both registers — uses the same 2px --wood-line border at 14px radius. Only the fill changes. A violet panel stops reading as a foreign object on brown the moment it is seated in the same frame as the board cards: it becomes a window cut into the board, not a sticker on top of it.

Where each register is used

Board — Home, streak, Hero Status Check, quest cards, plaques, exercise cards, tab bar, all nav chrome. Anything you act on now.
Vellum — the Adventure Map / Walking Tracker, Plan's weekly quest map, Wins & history, milestone tracks. Anything that depicts elapsed distance or time.
The rule of thumb: if it charts a journey, it's vellum; if it's a thing on the board, it's wood.

One gold, not two

The app currently defines two near-identical golds. They should collapse to a single token. Proposed value is the midpoint — warm enough for wood, bright enough to stay legible on violet.
